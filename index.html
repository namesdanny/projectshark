Intro


Humans fear the danger of sharks so much so, that certain jurisdictions such as Australia and Hawaii release culling policies. Culling policies are systematic capturing and killing of sharks through drum lines and specified kill zones. These operations are only effective of enhancing beach safety in theory and have never proven. Not only this, but culling is extremely indiscriminate so other species such as dolphins, turtles, and seals also get killed in the process. Above is an interview with Skyler Thomas, a wildlife film maker and founder of White Shark Video. Best known for his feature length film that won over 20 awards in various film festivals, Great White Lies, Skyler Thomas teaches people to understand sharks and change their perspective about them through entertaining films. He believes the best way to get rid of fear of sharks and understand them is to spend time with them and learn more about them. .


Ultimately, sharks are indeeed impressive creatures with advanced scenses and sharp teeth. However, humans need to understand that they are rea`lly just animals trying to search for food and stay alive, and humans are not on their list. There have been etremely low numbers of shark related casualties. Attacks are never done on purpose to kill a human. Sharks attack under certain natural instincts such as protecting territory or mistaking humans as their main food source. As humans, we need to understand that the ocean isn't solely ours, rather, we are like the many other animals in the ecosystem all trying to survive either way. In order to stay safe in the water from sharks, we need to be aware of shark bahaviors and understand their characteristics. We cannot kill a species for a playing their natural roles in life. Instead of culling there are many other alternatives there are many other alternatives to ensuring safety in the ocean in case of the odd times that a shark may attack.
