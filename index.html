Intro


Humans fear the danger of sharks so much so, that certain jurisdictions such as Australia and Hawaii release culling policies. Culling policies are systematic capturing and killing of sharks through drum lines and specified kill zones. These operations are only effective of enhancing beach safety in theory and have never proven. Not only this, but culling is extremely indiscriminate so other species such as dolphins, turtles, and seals also get killed in the process. Above is an interview with Skyler Thomas, a wildlife film maker and founder of White Shark Video. Best known for his feature length film that won over 20 awards in various film festivals, Great White Lies, Skyler Thomas teaches people to understand sharks and change their perspective about them through entertaining films. He believes the best way to get rid of fear of sharks and understand them is to spend time with them and learn more about them. .


Ultimately, sharks are indeeed impressive creatures with advanced scenses and sharp teeth. However, humans need to understand that they are really just animals trying to search for food and stay alive, and humans are not on their list. There have been etremely low numbers of shark related casualties. Attacks are never done on purpose to kill a human. Sharks attack under certain natural instincts such as protecting territory or mistaking humans as their main food source. As humans, we need to understand that the ocean isn't solely ours, rather, we are like the many other animals in the ecosystem all trying to survive either way. In order to stay safe in the water from sharks, we need to be aware of shark bahaviors and understand their characteristics. We cannot kill a species for a playing their natural roles in life. Instead of culling there are many other alternatives there are many other alternatives to ensuring safety in the ocean in case of the odd times that a shark may attack.

Safety Alternatves

Why We Shouldnt Cull Sharks

Shark culling hasn't shown any proof of decreasing shark bites after action. For example, the Hawaii shark cull reported a little to no decrease in attacks after killing over 4,500 sharks. Sharks are an extremely important part of the ocean. As apex predators, sharks single out the weak in an ecosystem, preventing them from spreading their inferior genes. As well as apex predators, sharks are scavengers who remove dead animals from the sea before they cause contamination and spread of disease.

As shark populations decrease, other populations of species falter which effect an ecosystem's tropic cascade. This in turn effects food sources that we rely on as well as other species. As our food sources decrease, businesses that rely on that food source also begin to dwindle and the chain reactions continue. Instead of culling, there are many other options to stay safe in the ocean and maintain peace of mind in the rare case that a shark may attack.


Shark Repellants

There are several shark types of shark repellants, magnetic (work similar to electromagnetic), pheromone (uses dead shark pheromones to ward off sharks), and the most common, electromagnetic. While they may be a bit pricey, these repellants have undertaken a lot of research and proof of effectiveness. These are available at Ocean Guardian for divers, fishermen, and surfers.


Sharks have electromagnetic sensors in their snouts that they use to help detect prey and various obstacles in the ocean. The way electromagnetic repellants work is by emitting strong electrodes that overload the shark's senses causing muscle spasms which drive the shark away without causing harm. Personal Change and Insight

Steps towards becoming more safe from a shark attack can be inexpensive and simple as well. For example, a simple change in swim wear or diving gear. Sharks best see contrasting colors so switching from bright patterned swim wear to something dark blue, like the ocean, can help you be less noticeable to sharks. As well as swim wear, changing board color if you are a surfer as well as boat color can also prevent sharks from seeing your board and mistaking it for a seal.

Another good thing to know is what circumstances might mean sharks are nearby. Places like sandbars and sudden drop offs in the ocean are common place for sharks. If flocks of seabird or dolphins are nearby that may mean sharks are too. This is because dolphins and seabird feed on bait fish which may also attract a shark. Sewage drop offs also attract baitfish which in turn attract feeding sharks. Take note of such situations and be able to understand what is and isn't safe.
